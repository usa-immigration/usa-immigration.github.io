Why Immigration Issues Matter


Scientists, Engineers, and Innovators

Many immigrants hold college degrees, and are highly educated. According to the Census Bureau, 33 percent of the immigrant population with a bachelor's degree or higher are engineers while 24 percent are physical scientists. The Partnership for a New American Economy reports that of the 10 top patent-producing universities, over 75% were issued to foreign-born inventors in 2011. Additionally, around 25 percent of public companies have been founded by immigrants. These companies include Google, eBay, Yahoo!, Intel, and many more. Business Owners

The United States Census Bureau reports that there are roughly 27.9 million businesses in the country. A significant portion of these businesses are immigrant-owned. About 18 percent of the small businesses are owned by immigrants. Not only do immigrants own a large portion of the businesses but they are also 30% more likely to start their own business than non-immigrants.



Create Jobs for American Workers

Despite what many people believe, immigrants create jobs for American workers rather than steal them. According to the Fiscal Policy Institute, small businesses owned by immigrants employ around 4.7 million people and these businesses generate more than $776 billion in revenue per year. Research done by Professor Ahmed Mushfiq Mobarak, Professor of Economics at Yale University, found other ways that immigration benefits the American economy. For instance, immigrants taking a low paying job, such as washing the dishes in a restaurant, makes the restaurant more sustainable and viable. In the long run, this allows the owner to hire more workers, thus creating more jobs for American workers.
