Current Policies in the United States

Family based

Immediate relatives or spouses are admitted as US citizens if their family member is a citizen Every year the number of visas given varies. The lowest number the United States is permitted to give is 226,000, but realistically there are over 480,000 given. Family members are giving preferences (children, parents, and spouses)

Employment based

Immigrants that have valuable skills are given visas Temporary and permanent visas given More than 20 types temporary visas 140,000 permanent visas are given a year. This number includes the workers and their families. For permanent visas, there are 5 preference categories. Depending on how valuable the United States deems your ability, the higher preference you are.

Refugees/ Asylees

The United States grants asylum to individuals who are fleeing persecution due to their race, religion, nationality, or political opinion. In 2015, the United States granted asylum to over 26,000 immigrants. This is a small fraction of the number of refugees who apply for asylum. They apply for admission from a transition country. The admission is based off of the degree of risk the person faces. The max number of refugees accepted is decided by the president. To put the ceiling number in perspective, in 2016 there were about 85,000 refugees admitted. Asylees apply to stay in the United States while they are already in the United States. They can apply at the port of entry, or within one year of their entry. There is no maximum number of Asylees the United States accepts every year.
